Do you possess the ability to do the work of a Maintenance and Operations Supervisor without more than normal supervision? This includes but is not limited to experience allocating resources and distributes work to the Boiler Plant, Carpentry, Electricians, HVAC, Painting, Plumbing, Pipefitters, Locksmith, and Maintenance Helper units.
 I have the ability to supervise employees as a Maintenance Operations Supervisor. I can perform the most complex and difficult tasks associated with all functions of a Maintenance Supervisor position. I have experience as a supervisor and proven ability to read and interpret technical directives, blueprints, sketches, mechanical diagrams and electrical schematics to visualize all phases of the work to be performed and to draw upon past experience and other sources for solution of planning problems, Ability to accurately estimate manhours and material requirements and to plan workloads, operate a computer to generate required reports. Must understand and be able to manipulate computer files in order to compile reports. Examples of duties include planning and scheduling work assignments on immediate and long-range basis for accomplishment by the maintenance shop, evaluating section effectiveness, cost production and other work projects for which he/she is responsible, providing information, advice, and recommendations to superiors, and coordinating and scheduling work with other supervisors.
 I have the ability to supervise employees as a Maintenance Supervisor. I have non-supervisory experience working independently with limited supervision performing the full range of Maintenance Operations duties and possess the ability to perform the duties of a supervisor but lack experience and training. Examples of duties include performing duties of the supervisor when the supervisor is absent and maintaining all phases of the job in an acceptable manner or successfully take charge or lead a small group of workers to complete a specific assignment.
 I have the ability to supervise employees as a Maintenance Supervisor. I have only limited knowledge performing routine Maintenance Operations Supervisor techniques, procedures and functions, under normal supervision, and have not had the experience or opportunity to lead or supervise.
 I do not have the ability to lead employees as a Maintenance Supervisor.
