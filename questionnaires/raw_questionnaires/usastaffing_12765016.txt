Specialized Experience: You must have at least one (1) full year of specialized experience, equivalent to the next lower grade level (GS-5) in Federal Service, that equipped you with the particular knowledge, skills, and abilities to successfully perform the duties of a Surgical Technician, and that is typically in or related to the work of the position to be filled. Qualifying specialized experience includes:
Adherence to aseptic technique
Operates of a variety of instrumentation and service lines including orthopedic, general urology, ophthalmology, vascular, thoracic and plastic, surgical ethics and consciousness
Adaptable to a variety of caseloads, self-starter and time management skills,
Establishes and maintains sterile field during surgical procedures
Assist circulating nurses and surgical team in maintaining sterile environment
Assist circulating nurses in gathering surgical supplies and equipment
Track physician preference cards accurately and updated as required
Confirm all specimens to be sent to pathology with surgeon and verify accuracy with team, gown, and gloves members of surgical team
Transport all surgical equipment to appropriate cleaning areas
Assist in daily instruments and supply organization
Attend conferences, huddles, meetings as required
Communicate effectively and courteously with visitors, physicians, office staff, employees, and patients.
Maintain designated certificates as required
