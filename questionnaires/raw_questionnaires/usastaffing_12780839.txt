Assessment 1
Thank you for your interest in employment with the Department of Veterans Affairs. The following assessment questionnaire is used to determine if you meet the Basic Requirements and the Grade Requirements for this vacancy. Please choose the most appropriate answer in response to the following questions.
BASIC REQUIREMENTS: The basic requirements for employment as a VHA Physical Therapist are prescribed by the Physical Therapist Qualification Standard - GS-0633 (formerly VA Handbook 5005/99, Part II, Appendix G12 - February 27, 2018). To qualify for appointment as a Physical Therapist in VHA, all applicants must meet the following.
1. Basic Education and/or Experience - Please select your highest level of education and experience from the options below.
(a) Bachelor's degree in Physical Therapy AND five (5) years of progressively independent experience as a physical therapist. -OR-
(b) Master's Degree in Physical Therapy AND two (2) years of progressively independent experience as a physical therapist.-OR-
(c) Doctorate degree in physical therapy.

PLEASE NOTE: Individuals must be a graduate of a Commission on Accreditation in Physical Therapy Education (CAPTE) accredited college or university.

Foreign Graduates - Graduates of foreign physical therapy programs meet the requirements if they have a current, full, active, and unrestricted license to practice physical therapy in a State, Territory or Commonwealth of the United States, or in the District of Columbia.

You must submit a copy of your Transcripts with your application. The copy of Transcripts may be official or unofficial, however should contain Your Name, the Institutions Name, City and State as well as the Degree earned and the DATE the degree was conferred.

Do you meet the education and/or experience requirements as indicated above?
 A. I have a Bachelor's degree in physical therapy and five (5) years of progressively independent experience as an physical therapist.
 B. I have a Master's degree in physical therapy and two (2) years of progressively independent experience as an physical therapist.
 C. I have a Doctorate degree in physical therapy.
 D. I am a Foreign Graduate and possess a current, full, active, and unrestricted license to practice physical therapy in a State, Territory or Commonwealth of the United States, or in the District of Columbia.
 E. I do not possess the required education and or experience required for this position.
2. Licensure - You must hold a full, current, and unrestricted license to practice physical therapy in a State, Territory, or Commonwealth of the United States, or the District of Columbia.

EXCEPTION: Non-licensed physical therapists (PT's), who otherwise meet the basic requirements, may be given a temporary appointment as a graduate PT at the GS-11 grade level for a period not-to-exceed two years.

Do you possess a full, current, and unrestricted license, to practice physical therapy in a state, territory, or commonwealth of the United States (i.e., Puerto Rico), or in the District of Columbia -OR- will you be fully licensed within the next two (2) years?
 A. Yes, I am fully licensed to practice physical therapy in a state, territory, or commonwealth of the United States (i.e., Puerto Rico), or in the District of Columbia
 B. No, I am NOT fully licensed. However, I am pursuing licensure and intend on becoming fully licensed within the next two (2) years.
 C. No, I am NOT fully licensed, NOR will I be able to obtain licensure within the next two (2) years and acknowledge my application may not receive further consideration.

Do you possess ALL the GS-12 Grade Requirements for Experience, Education, AND Licensure?

 B. No, I do NOT possess ALL the required experience, education, or licensure to be appointed at the GS-12 level.
Select “Yes” or “No” to the following question(s).
5. GS-12 Knowledge, Skills, & Abilities (KSAs): In addition to the Basic Requirements, a Physical Therapist at the full performance GS-12 level MUST have demonstrated possession of the required knowledge, skills, and abilities associated with the scope of physical therapy practice as follows:
Ability to make autonomous clinical decisions in a Direct Access environment. This includes ability to independently evaluate and treat patients who seek physical therapy services.
Skill in performing examinations and evaluations of individuals who have or may develop impairments, activity limitations, and participation restrictions related to conditions of the musculoskeletal, neuromuscular, cardiovascular, pulmonary, and/or integumentary systems while considering the effects attributable to unique psychosocial and environmental factors.
Ability to interpret findings from examination and evaluation, select appropriate test and measures, and integrate findings into the physical therapy plan of care for the full range of patient populations.
Ability to establish a diagnosis within the scope of physical therapy and identify the appropriate rehabilitation intervention, including referral to another provider for further consultation as clinically indicated.
Ability to determine physical therapy prognosis by incorporating examination findings with the patient's preferences in order to set clinically appropriate treatment goals, optimize outcomes and maximize functional independence.
Ability to independently provide clinical oversight of Physical Therapy Assistants as well as students on clinical affiliations who are in Doctoral PT Programs or PTA Programs.

Do you possess and/or can you demonstrate  ALL the KSAs at the GS-12 level?
 A. Yes
 B. No
