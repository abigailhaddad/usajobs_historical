Knowledge, Skills, and Abilities (KSAs): In addition to the experience, you must be able to demonstrate the following (KSAs) at the full performance level:
Ability to produce diagnostic quality images and Doppler frequency shift recording using M-mode, tow dimensional, pulsed and continuous wave Doppler special display, and color flow Doppler imaging.
Knowledge of normal echocardiographic findings and deviations produced b a wide variety of cardiovascular diseases.
Ability to perform complex procedures such as cardiac studies of the four chambers of the heart and valves using sector scanning techniques to detect stenotic and incompetent valves, chamber enlargement, and pericardial effusion and cardiovascular studies deriving pressure gradients across obstructed valves, etc.
Ability to perform complementary ultrasonic examinations (including noninvasive) transthoracic, Bubble studies and contrast echocardiography, (invasive) stress echocardiography, and transesophageal echocardiography.
Ability to adapt transducer positioning, instrument controls, and examinations techniques to the individual patient being studied and the problem being evaluated including recognizing pertinent abnormalities and documenting abnormal findings.
