Assessment 1
This section will be used to determine if you possess the experience needed to qualify for an Aircraft Engine Mechanic, WG-8602-10 position. Failure to respond to this question will result in an ineligible rating.
1. Select the one statement which best describes the highest level of experience you possess and which demonstrates your ability to perform the duties of an Aircraft Engine Mechanic, WG-8602-10 without more than normal supervision. Note: Your resume must support the response you select.
 A. I am able to perform the most difficult and complex tasks on one or more types or models of malfunctioning conventional and/or modified aircraft engines on the flight line, in test cells or maintenance shops. I am able to repair, test, and modify experimental aircraft engines; plan, layout, carry through operation to complete jobs; adapt instrument and measurement procedures, interpret all specifications in locating answers for complex problems of troubleshooting, repair, etc.; trace and correct malfunctions in experimental engines which are considered to be complicated; work with incomplete or inaccurate references and specifications, adapting a thorough knowledge of trade theory and practices to solve the specific problems encountered. I am able to adapt emerging technology and use new tools, test devices and equipment such as digital diagnostic equipment, engine analysis software and specialized measuring devices. I am able to detect mismatched or misaligned parts as a possible cause of damage or malfunction. I have good knowledge of new developments in aircraft engine design and repair in order to suggest modification to experimental equipment to achieve design goals of operation, maintain ability, etc.
 B. I am able to perform a full range of aircraft engine mechanic tasks including the ability to perform all usual tasks required to test, adjust, troubleshoot, modify, maintain, repair and install a wide variety of aircraft reciprocating and jet engines and supporting electrical, mechanical, hydraulic and pneumatic accessory systems in order to troubleshoot malfunctions where first symptoms that may appear in a different area, for example, following back from observed improper combustion to determine if trouble is in the combustion chamber, compressor, fuel control unit, air intake, nozzle or other more remote causes. I am able to perform engine repair practices in order to identify and correctly choose between alternate methods and trade techniques to adapt accepted repair procedures to new and unfamiliar engines or accessory systems, to anticipate what tools and parts will be required and to set up the work area.
 C. I am able to perform common aircraft engine mechanic tasks. I work under the normal supervision of a work leader or journey-level performing limited assembly of components and less complex modules such as compressors; perform routine disassembly and limited assembly duties independently; perform visual, dimensional, and tactile checks of individual parts for foreign object damage, stress fractures, excessive wear, etc; assemble subassemblies of limited complexity (e.g., rotors, stator cases, combustion chambers, and augmentors/ afterburners) in accordance with specific procedures and requirements while under limited supervision. I have knowledge of engine disassembly practices as sequences of disassembly of engines or components to allow best use of tools and prevent damage to parts or injury to self.
 D. I am able to perform simple aircraft engine mechanic duties such as disassemble subassemblies and recognize damaged parts and 100 percent replacement items which should be discarded, using a general knowledge of mechanical parts and assemblies to understand the relationship of the various parts and recognize such conditions as missing parts, burred edge or scored shafts, to be reported to higher graded employees. I am able to use proper type and size of tools and perform assigned work tasks.
 E. My experience does not match any of the statement described above.
