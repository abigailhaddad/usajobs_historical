IF NOT, YOU WILL BE FOUND INELIGIBLE FOR THIS POSITION OR YOUR SCORE WILL BE LOWERED. The level of these skills are typically gained after three or more seasons of leading a professional trail crew.

 A. I have experience leading crews of more than 4 persons performing complex backcountry trail maintenance and trail structure construction in remote settings for long periods of time. I have led crews in the reconstruction of log check runs, rock and log retaining walls, boardwalks and raised turnpikes/causeways, switchback layout and construction, revegetation efforts, and I have also led crews in the set up and use of complex rigging systems and the protocols of backcountry work camps. These duties were completed while working on a professional trail crew (National Park Service, U.S. Forest Service, etc). The level of these skills are typically gained after three or more seasons of leading a professional trail crew.
 B. I have experience leading a trail crew of 2 or more persons in remote settings, performing most aspects of trail maintenance and trail structure construction including at least three of the following: log check runs, retaining wall, boardwalk, raised turnpike/causeway or switchback construction, large scale trail clearing or brushing projects or large scale trail bed rehabilitation efforts including back slope, fill slope, and drainage on backcountry trails for a professional trail crew (National Park Service, U.S. Forest Service, etc). The level of these skills are typically gained after two or more seasons of leading a professional trail crew.
 C. I have experience leading a backcountry trail crew while performing basic trail maintenance for a professional trail crew (National Park Service, U.S. Forest Service, state parks, etc). During that full season, I regularly led a crew of one or more crew members clearing trees and brush from the trail corridor with power saws, installing and maintaining trail structures including water bars, erosion checks, and making minor repairs to backcountry campground structures such as pit toilets and benches or stock facilities such as fences, hitch rails and loading ramps.
 D. I have experience working as a professional backcountry trail crew member (National Park Service, U.S. Forest Service, etc.) performing basic trail maintenance including clearing trees and brush from the trail corridor with power saws, maintaining trail structures including drainage devices and erosion checks, and making minor repairs to backcountry campground structures or stock facilities. I do not have experience leading crews or trail projects.
 E. I do not have any backcountry trail crew maintenance experience.
