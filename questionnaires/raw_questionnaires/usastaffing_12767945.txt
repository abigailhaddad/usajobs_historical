Assessment 1
This section will be used to determine if you possess the experience required to perform the duties of a Materials Examiner Identifier (Fork Lift Operator), WG-6912-07 position. Failure to respond to this question will result in an ineligible rating.
1. Select the one statement that best describes your highest level of experience as it relates to your ability to perform the duties of a Materials Examiner Identifier (Fork Lift Operator), WG-6912-07, without more than normal supervision. Your resume must reflect the experience you select.
 A. I am able to perform the most difficult and complex tasks of a Materials Examiner Identifier (Fork Lift Operator). I have the ability to receive, examine, verify, and identify unclassified, classified, hazardous, sensitive and Arms Ammunitions & Explosive (AA&E) materials and equipment; performs comprehensive research of shipping and storage records, equipment specifications, and manufacturing manuals as required for item/equipment identification.
 B. I am able to perform the full range of tasks of a Materials Examiner Identifier (Fork Lift Operator). I have the ability to perform a broad range of standard traffic management in-checking assignments to include identifying hazardous, classified, AA&E, and sensitive cargo requiring special handling during in-checking.
 C. I am able to perform the common tasks of a Materials Examiner Identifier (Fork Lift Operator). I have the ability to reviews Government Bills of Lading (GBL) and inspects shipments for any discrepancies to AA&E, classified, hazardous or general cargo and ensures data is correct, properly identified, packaged, marked and labeled.
 D. Under close supervision, I am able to perform simple tasks or I have received training on material handling tasks involving receiving, storing, selecting, inventory and shipping specialized materials and equipment.
 E. My experience as a Materials Examiner Identifier (Fork Lift Operator) does not match any of the statements above.
