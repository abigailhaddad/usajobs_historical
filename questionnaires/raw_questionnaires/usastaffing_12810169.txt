Specialized experience: Examples of specialized experience would typically include, but are not limited to: Requires intensive knowledge and experience of standard supply management procedures to perform work involving Inventory and excess material management. Utilizes a thorough knowledge of and ability to interpret. policies, procedures, manuals, and regulations pertaining to supply/inventory management and federal acquisition systems to ensure team conformance with established requirements. Requires thorough knowledge of related stock rotation, hazard alerts and recalls ensuring outdated or otherwise compromised supplies and material are removed from usage area. Must have advanced knowledge and experience of an automated point of use inventory system (OmniCell or similar secure dispensing system), as well as the parent inventory system, both of which monitor issues and receipts in support of stock replenishment processes. The interface between the two can be a significant source of error. Incumbent must be able to evaluate numerical differences between the two systems and reconcile. Requires analytical ability to define and recognize problems, to collect applicable data and make recommendations for a solution. Thorough knowledge¬†and experience of an automated computerized supply/inventory management system and familiarity with Microsoft (MS) Office software programs such as MS Word and Excel. Computer skills are required related to entering information and management of supply inventory systems using the generic inventory package and validate the data provided by the automated system, due to errors caused by frequent customer misunderstanding of the system's screen Instructions. Data accuracy in the automated system is critical to long-term stock management.
