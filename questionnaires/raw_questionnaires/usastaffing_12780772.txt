1. Knowledge and application of an extensive body of transportation regulations, methods, and industry practices to perform a wide variety of transportation support assignments and resolve a wide range of problems.
2. Knowledge of transportation regulations that relate to the movement of a wide range of specialized shipments (over-dimensional, hazardous, and security risk items).
3. Requires extensive practical knowledge of the carrier industry and specialized shipment needs to identify the appropriate shipping method, carrier equipment, services, and special handling required.

4. Skill in analyzing and interpreting technical transportation requirements in determining mode and carrier according to priority of assigned shipments.
5. Ability to work independently to ensure efficient and economical use of carrier service, monitor reports of shipment discrepancies, to locate lost cargo and assess liability for damaged cargo.
