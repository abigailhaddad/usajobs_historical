Assessment 1
This section will be used to determine if you possess the experience needed to qualify for an Aircraft Mechanic, WG-8852-11 position. Failure to respond to this question will result in an ineligible rating.
1. Select the one statement which best describes the highest level of experience you possess and which demonstrates your ability to perform the duties of an Aircraft Mechanic, WG-8852-11 without more than normal supervision. Note: Your resume must support the response you select.
 A. I am able to perform the most difficult and complex Aircraft Mechanic tasks. I have the skill in interpreting and applying written guidelines and able to analyze changes to those guidelines, as well as maintenance modification orders. I have the ability to perform complex task such as to install, adjust, align, troubleshoot and perform final functional and operational tests on a variety of major aircraft systems, their assemblies, and components such as airframe, landing gear, power plant, gear boxes, pumps, and hydraulic, pressurization, utility, fuel, oil, pneumatic, and flight control systems. I have theory, operation, and integration of all mechanical, pneudraulic, electrical, electronic and other operating systems of the assigned aircraft in order to troubleshoot malfunctions from pilots and flight crew reports of discrepancies, as well as from observations during inspections and run-ups. I am cognizant of the required safety precautions and skill full in trade responsibilities.
 B. I am able to perform the full range of Aircraft Mechanic tasks. I have the ability to receive oral or written instructions or work orders in order to install, adjust, align, troubleshoot and perform final functional and operational tests on a variety of major aircraft systems, their assemblies, and components such as airframe, landing gear, power plant, gear boxes, pumps, and hydraulic, pressurization, utility, fuel, oil, pneumatic, and flight control systems. I am able to debrief pilots and aircrew, and check operation logs for reports of malfunctions to determine if repairs are needed. I have the ability to use judgment to determine the tools, methods, and techniques to complete work assignments. I am cognizant of the required safety precautions and skill full in trade responsibilities.
 C. I am able to perform the common Aircraft Mechanic tasks. I have the ability to read and understand specific directions including disassembly, assembly, and adjustment directions for tasks such as replacing control cables and pulleys, lacing in fuel bladders, and reinstalling hydraulic lines, air ducts, and inspection plates. I have the ability to do repairs of limited complexity such as removing and replacing worn or damaged gaskets, pneudraulic lines and couplings, fuel cells, cables, and pulleys under close supervision. I am able to assist in retrieving a variety of parts and components such as gaskets, couplings, control cables, gear boxes and flight control surfaces. I am alert to the safety of self and others.
 D. I am able to perform the simple Aircraft Mechanic tasks. I have the ability to read and understand simple directions to perform simple aircraft mechanic task. I have the ability to assist with repairs of limited complexity such as removing and replacing worn or damaged gaskets, pneudraulic lines and couplings, fuel cells, cables, and pulleys under close supervision of higher graded employees. I have the ability to recognize a wide variety of parts and components such as gaskets, couplings, control cables, gear boxes and flight control surfaces with the help of higher graded employees. I am alert to the safety of self and others.
 E. My experience as an Aircraft Mechanic does not match any of the statements above.
