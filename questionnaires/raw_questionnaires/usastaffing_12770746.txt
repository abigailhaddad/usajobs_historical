Assessment 2
1. What is the best first step in logical troubleshooting of a non-working RF system?

 A. Replace the antenna
 B. Replace the power supply
 C. Check power and ground at the radio
 D. Clean all connectors
 E. Swap transmitters
2. When performing a continuity test to chassis as part of verifying a proper system ground, what is the expected reading for an intact ground path?

 A. Infinite resistance (open circuit)
 B. A specific voltage reading (e.g., 120V AC)
 C. A very low resistance reading (near 0 ohms)
 D. A fluctuating resistance value
3. What is the primary objective of conducting field tests on electronic systems equipment?

 A. To verify system performance and reliability under actual operating conditions
 B. To demonstrate the equipment's features to potential buyers in a controlled showroom environment
 C. To determine the aesthetic appeal of the equipment in various outdoor settings
 D. To calculate the total cost of ownership for the equipment over its lifespan
4. Which of the following are considered precision tools commonly used by electronics technicians? Select all that apply:

 A. Multimeter
 B. Oscilloscope
 C. Hammer
 D. Soldering Iron
5. Which of the following tasks are typically involved in preventive maintenance for field electronics? Select all that apply:
 A. Cleaning connectors
 B. Checking voltages
 C. Testing grounding points
 D. Reflowing all solder joints
 E. Replacing all chips annually
6. What piece of test equipment is most applicable to display and analyze
the waveforms of electrical signals?

 A. Digital Multimeter
 B. Oscilloscope
 C. Logic Analyzer
 D. RF Wattmeter
 E. Frequency Counter
7. Which of the following describes the nature of voltage in an electric field?

 A. It is directly proportional to the current flowing through a conductor
 B. It represents the total quantity or effect of the field through space
 C. It is the work needed per unit of charge to move a positive test charge between two points
 D. It is always constant regardless of the distance between two points
8. What is the primary purpose of calibration as a preventative maintenance task for electronic equipment?
 A. To remove dust and contaminants from circuit boards.
 B. To apply lubricants to moving parts to reduce friction.
 C. To adjust instruments and equipment to ensure accurate and consistent measurements.
 D. To replace components that have a known limited lifespan.
9. What is a simple way to describe the function of a capacitor in an electrical circuit?
 A. It acts like a switch to turn the circuit on or off.
 B. It stores electrical energy and releases it quickly.
 C. It increases the resistance of the circuit to control current flow.
 D. It converts electrical energy into light or heat.
10. What is the primary purpose of measuring Standing Wave Ratio (SWR) in an RF system?

 A. To determine the frequency range of operation
 B. To assess the efficiency of power transfer from the transmitter to the load
 C. To identify the type of modulation being used
 D. To measure the voltage and current at different points in the circuit
11. Which of the following is a common use of the Very High Frequency (VHF) band?

 A. Long-distance shortwave radio broadcasting
 B. Communication with submarines
 C. FM radio broadcasting
 D. Satellite communication
12. What does adjusting a component to the manufacturer's maximum attainable tolerance typically involve?

 A. Replacing the system software
 B. Ensuring output is within exact design specifications
 C. Adding external resistors to lower current flow
 D. Lowering input voltage below threshold
13. A workstation will not boot. What is the best first step in troubleshooting?
 A. Reset the CPU
 B. Replace the hard drive
 C. Check power cable and verify power to the system
 D. Reset the BIOS
 E. Remove the graphics card
14. Which of the following best describes proper maintenance of external computer peripherals like printers and modems?
 A. Resetting all devices every 30 days
 B. Cleaning connectors and verifying communication functions
 C. Running memory-intensive tests monthly
 D. Upgrading firmware during power-off states
15. When performing a diode test with a digital multimeter in diode test mode, what does an "OL" reading in both the forward and reverse bias directions typically indicate?
 A. A perfectly functioning diode with no voltage drop.
 B. A shorted diode allowing current flow in both directions.
 C. An opened diode that is not allowing current flow in either direction.
 D. A diode with a very high forward voltage drop.
16. During a detailed inspection of malfunctioning communications equipment, what is your primary objective?
 A. Document component serial numbers for the asset inventory
 B. Replace all components regardless of performance
 C. Identify the root cause and determine precise corrective action
 D. Wait for software patches to auto-install
