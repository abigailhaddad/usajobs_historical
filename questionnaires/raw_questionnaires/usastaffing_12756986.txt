This section will be used to determine if you possess the experience needed to qualify for an Electronic Integrated Systems Mechanic, WG-2610-12 position. Failure to respond to this question will result in an ineligible rating.
1. Select the one statement which best describes the highest level of experience you possess and which demonstrates your ability to perform the duties of an Electronic Integrated Systems Mechanic, WG-2610-12 position without more than normal supervision. Note: Your resume must support the response you select.
 A. I am able to perform the most difficult and complex Electronic Integrated Systems Mechanic tasks. I have the ability to plan and perform maintenance and repair of a complete multisystem composed of a number of individual electronic integrated systems which are extensively interconnected. I have the ability to troubleshoot, adjust, and align all elements of the multisystem complex using extensive practical knowledge of electronics, computer logic, and electrohydraulic controls and their relationships in the complex in order to determine the effects that changes in one integrated systems will have on all the other systems with which it is connected. I’m cognizant and capable of instructing fellow coworkers on safety precautions. I have the ability to use and instruct others on how to use hand and power tools such as O-scopes, pulse generators, power supplies, resistance decades, electrostatic field meters, digital/analog multimeters, precision measurement tools and test equipment, etc.
 B. I am able to perform a full range of Electronic Integrated Systems Mechanic tasks. I have the ability to plan and perform maintenance and repair of individual electronic integrated systems such as radar navigation systems. I have the ability to troubleshoot the system, tracing back the various malfunction indications through interrelated units of the systems to locate the source of the deficiency. I’m cognizant and capable of instructing fellow coworkers on safety precautions. I have the ability to use and instruct others on how to use hand and power tools such as O-scopes, pulse generators, power supplies, resistance decades, electrostatic field meters, digital/analog multimeters, precision measurement tools and test equipment, etc.
 C. I am able to perform common Electronic Integrated Systems Mechanic tasks. I have the ability to either independently or as member of a team, to install, overhaul, maintain, and repair a variety of complete operational electronic equipment such as radar, sonar, computer, or navigational aids. I have knowledge of theory of operation of the foregoing equipment and specialized circuitry used, as well as a broad general background in electronic theory and the ability to apply these knowledges in repair operations. I have the ability to use basic hand tools in order to repair electronic equipment. I’m cognizant on safety precautions.
 D. I am able to perform simple Electronic Integrated Systems Mechanic tasks. I have the ability to perform simple task such as repairing a variety of operational electronic equipment with the assistance of higher graded team members. I have the ability to use basic hand tools in order to repair electronic equipment under the close supervision. I’m cognizant on safety precautions.
 E. My ability to perform as an Electronic Integrated Systems Mechanic does not match any of the statements above.
