1. ABILITY TO DO THE WORK OF A MAINTENANCE WORKER (TRAILS) WITHOUT MORE THAN NORMAL SUPERVISION (SCREEN OUT) from the descriptions below, please choose the best response to describe your ability to perform maintenance worker (trails) work. You must document your experience in your resume.
 A. I have worked as a maintenance worker performing and leading others in trail rehabilitation. This includes work such as building, maintaining and repairing trail tread and drainage structures, constructing dry and wet stone multi-coursed/tiered rock walls, turnpiking, cribbing, and foot bridges, removing windfalls, felling trees, and utilizing block, tackle, and winches for moving and placing heavy objects. I am proficient at installing/repairing/cleaning water bars- rock and wood, turnpiking, and repairing trail tread through leveling with fill. I am proficient with cross cuts and chainsaws. I can lift and carry objects weighing more than 50 pounds and hike up to 20 miles a day over uneven terrain.
 B. I have worked as a maintenance worker performing trail work with limited supervision. I have performed routine trail maintenance functions such as water bar cleaning, clearing trails of windfalls, leveling tread, and brushing with minimal supervision. I am proficient using a chainsaw in felling timber, cutting, bucking, and limbing trees.
 C. I have performed maintenance worker tasks on a trail crew or group responsible for carrying out work on trails with no more than normal supervision. I have constructed rock walls, water bars, and retainer bars. I have used chainsaws to cut wood or limb trees.
 D. I have not performed the maintenance worker tasks as described above.
