1. Wage Grade 4: From the descriptions listed below, select the response that best describes your experience as a Food Service Worker. This includes your ability to perform the duties of a Food Service Worker without more than normal supervision, but is not limited to: proficiency in special food storage, safety and sanitation procedures and a broad knowledge of food service and food production operations such as food preparation and serving including special procedures and principles necessary in the preparation of tube feedings, dry and refrigerated storage, the NFS online ordering system, or equivalent, for outpatient areas and proper food handling, storage and rotation per the Hazard Analysis Critical Control Point (HACCP) program to prevent biological, chemical and foreign body contamination and subsequent food borne illness. Must be skilled in the use of automated data processing equipment and systems, knowledge and understanding of food/drug interactions and food allergens, Knowledgeable in methods and procedures relating to food preparations in small quantities and the skill to apply such information to cook a limited variety of prepared foods, knowledgeable of the characteristics of the various foods such as color, flavor, consistency texture and temperature and cooking time required, skilled in planning, coordinating, and timing the sequence of steps required to have their menu items ready for serving at meal time. This is a screen-out element. Failure to meet a minimum level will result in an ineligible rating. Select only ONE letter.
