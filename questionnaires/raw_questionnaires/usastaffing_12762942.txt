 A. I have at least one year of specialized experience equivalent to at least the GS-06 level in the federal service. Specialized experience is experience that equipped the applicant with the particular knowledge, skills, and abilities to successfully perform the duties of the position, and that is typically in or related to the position to be filled. Examples of specialized experience include: Conduct excavation and testing of sites, including the careful recovery of sample materials. Duties include archeological monitoring, survey and excavation; recording data and preparing records; and maintaining related databases, forms, maps (including GIS), and files. Planning with team members and park cooperators in archeological assessments, surveys, and monitoring, as well as implementing archeological monitoring protocols. Working as a team member on complex projects such as restoration or mitigation actions on an archeological site. Conducts record searches and interviews, and synthesizes information collected for scientific, historical, and interpretive values. This position is also responsible for disseminating information about cultural resources to park staff, visitors, and the archeological community.
