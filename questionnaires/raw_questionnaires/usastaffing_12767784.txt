I have the ability to perform the most difficult and complex lock operations such as: serving as a shift lead at a lock and dam facility during assigned shift; being responsible for project security and the use of the lock by issuing instructions to tow boat captains and other craft operators; setting of precedence of entry; and maintaining proper procedural operation. I am skilled in determining the specific actions to take during breakdowns of equipment, delays in operation, and accidents; relaying information or passing on instructions to the next shift as appropriate; and utilizing a computer to input lock usage data. I am considered an expert and work with a high degree of independence.
 B. I have the ability to perform the full range of lock operations tasks such as: operating lock and dam equipment where lock and dam gates and valves are electrically or hydraulically controlled, providing locking instructions to others when needed, determining whether craft desiring lockage can be locked safely. I am skilled in reading, interpreting and following operating instructions for most equipment without supervisory assistance; recognizing and avoiding potential work hazards; and performing repairs necessary to keep the lock in operation. I work on my own initiative under general supervision subject to occasional inspection.
 C. I have the ability to perform some common lock operations tasks such as: operating lock and dam equipment where lock and dam gates and valves are electrically or hydraulically controlled, as directed. I can make minor repairs to lock and dam equipment; apply locking procedures, policies, and laws regarding traffic right-of-way, sequence of operations and safe passage of hazardous commodities and damaged crafts; and comply with governing safety practices and procedures. I work under normal supervision of a work leader or journey-level employee who observes tasks in progress and upon completion to make sure they are properly performed.
