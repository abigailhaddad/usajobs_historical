Assessment 1
1. From the description below select the one response that best describes how your experience/training meets the ability to do the work of a Mobile and Equipment Mechanic WG-5801-10 without more than normal supervision.
 A. I have independently overhauled and repaired major components of heavy and light equipment, including bulldozers, graders, dump trucks, front-end loaders, excavators, marine engines, generators, and small engine tools. My experience includes rebuilding engines, transmissions, and differentials; replacing crankshafts, timing systems, and drivetrain components; and troubleshooting and repairing hydraulic, electrical, and brake systems. I have diagnosed complex mechanical issues, performed system-level tests, and completed repairs requiring knowledge of how mechanical and electrical systems interact.
 B. I have substantial experience performing repairs on heavy trucks, equipment, and automotive vehicles in a full service auto shop. I have independently diagnosed and repaired issues involving ignition, cooling, and brake systems. I have assisted with or performed moderate overhauls such as replacing cylinder heads, timing components, and drivetrain parts. I have worked independently and on my own initiative and can complete most repairs without supervision.
 C. I have experience performing common tasks under general direction in a full automotive service shop on heavy trucks, equipment, and automotive vehicles. I have performed most of the following: replaced spark plugs, replaced water hoses and installed water pumps; replaced brake pads, replaced accessory belts and changed the oil on gas and diesel engines. I complete routine repairs independently and may require guidance for complex diagnostics or overhauls.
 D. I have some experience performing simple repairs on vehicles under direct supervision. I do not have the experience or on-the-job training to perform these tasks without more than normal supervision.
 E. Failed to reply.
