Thank you for your interest in the Electrician, WG-2805-10 position with the United States Coast Guard. Your resume and the responses you provide to this assessment questionnaire will be used to determine if you are among the best qualified for this position. Your responses are subject to verification. 1. To qualify for the Electrician, WG-2805-10, you must meet the following requirements: Applicants must meet the following Screen-Out element, Job Element #1: Ability to do the work of an Electrician without more than normal supervision as an Electrician, WG-2805-10 (SCREEN OUT). These duties include:

Applicants must have experience and/or training of sufficient scope and quality to independently perform the duties typically required of an Electrician at the journeyman level. These duties require skill in troubleshooting and repairing equipment, in marine (maritime), industrial, institutional, or commercial settings, providing the full range of electrical support in the areas of installation, diagnosis, repairing of electrical wiring, pulling of wires, and all other preventative maintenance. Have repaired/troubleshoot/diagnosis marine (maritime) generators(genset) and Transformers.

From the response listed below, please select the best response which demonstrates your Ability to do the work of an Electrician without more than normal supervision as an Electrician, WG-2805-10.
 A. I am able to perform the most difficult and complex Electrician tasks relating to the maintenance, troubleshooting, installation and repair in marine (maritime), industrial, institutional, and commercial settings, utilizing equipment such as web and sheet fed presses, binding equipment, robotic stackers, flatbed cutters, and commercial shop equipment used by all trades. I am consulted by other employees in difficult situations regarding the electrical repair and testing of equipment used in industrial, institutional, and commercial settings. The use of such equipment requires planning, lay-out, modifying, testing, and troubleshooting of electrical lines, circuits, and systems in an industrial, institutional, or commercial setting. I am considered an expert and work with a high degree of independence.
 B. I am able to perform the full range of Electrical tasks needed for repairing and testing in marine (maritime), industrial, institutional, and commercial settings, utilizing equipment such as web and sheet fed presses, binding equipment, robotic stackers, flatbed cutters, motors and associated controls enabling the installation and repairing of electrical circuits and systems utilized in industrial, institutional, and commercial areas. I work on my own initiative under general supervision subject to occasional inspection.
 C. I am able to perform common Electrician tasks in marine (maritime), industrial, institutional, and commercial settings utilizing equipment such as web and sheet fed presses, binding equipment, robotic stackers, flatbed cutters, and commercial shop equipment used by all trades. This includes routing and connecting wiring to electrical or electronic systems, components and equipment. I determine the routing location, type, size, gauges, and load for industrial, institutional, and commercial settings in shops and work areas. I work under close supervision of a work leader or journeyman level Electrician who observes tasks in progress and upon completion make sure they are properly performed.
 D. I am able to perform simple Electrician tasks, but I know little or nothing about electrician work.
 E. I do not have on the job training or experience as an Electrician.
