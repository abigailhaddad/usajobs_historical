Assessment 1
Thank you for your interest in a position with the Orlando VA Healthcare System. Your responses to this assessment questionnaire, in conjunction with the other portions of your completed application, will be evaluated in making selection decisions. When answering the questionnaire, remember that your experience is subject to verification by investigation. You may be asked to provide specific examples or documentation of experience as proof to support your answers, or you may be required to verify a response by a practical demonstration of your claimed ability to perform a task.

CREDITABLE EXPERIENCE: To be creditable, the experience must have required the use of knowledge, skills, abilities and other characteristics associated with current Medical Instrument Technician Echocardiography practice. This would be experience which demonstrates possession of the knowledge required in order to provide all aspects of echocardiography services.
GRADE REQUIREMENTS: Medical Instrument Technicians at GS-9 Echocardiography will have a functional assignment and reference should be made to the appropriate appendix for qualifications evaluation.
Select “Yes” or “No” to the following question(s).
1. Do you have at least 1 full year of experience comparable to the next lower grade level GS-8 which demonstrates the knowledge, skills, and abilities related to the duties of the position to be filled? This would be experience which provided the overall knowledge, skills, and abilities (KSAs) to conduct diagnostic echocardiography examinations in complex and unusual cases. GS-8 KSAs include: Ability to produce diagnostic quality images and Doppler frequency shift recordings using M-mode, two-dimensional, pulsed and continuous wave Doppler spectral display, and colorflow Doppler imaging. Knowledge of normal echocardiographic findings and deviations produced by a wide variety of cardiovascular diseases. Ability to perform complex procedures such as cardiac studies of the four chambers of the heart and valves using sector scanning techniques to detect stenotic and incompetent valves, chamber enlargement, and pericardial effusion and cardiovascular studies deriving pressure gradients across obstructed valves, etc. Ability to perform complementary ultrasonic examinations (including noninvasive) transthoracic, Bubble studies and contrast echocardiography, (invasive) stress echocardiography, and transesophageal echocardiography. Ability to adapt transducer positioning, instrument controls, and examination techniques to the individual patient being studied and the problem being evaluated including recognizing pertinent abnormalities and documenting abnormal findings.

Do you possess above experience?
