Select ONE appropriate response based on your experience and/or training that demonstrates your ability to perform the duties of this Animal Caretaker position at the WG-5 grade level without more than normal supervision.  A. I have practical knowledge of rearing fish in a hatchery and perform all phases of hatchery operations to care for eggs, fry, juvenile, and adult fish throughout the rearing and distribution cycle. I advise other animal caretakers on characteristics of hatchery fish or problems concerning their care. I am independently able to respond to emergency situations such as administering medication to fish, or adding chemicals to the water.
 B. I am able to perform the full range of animal caretaker fisheries tasks, from spawning to releases, on my own initiative, such as 1) recognizing observable signs of disease, discontent, or the general physical condition of fish; 2) in addition to knowing the normal feed requirements of fish, monitors fish and adjusts feed quantity, feeding cycles, and type of feed/supplements for current needs and circumstances, to increase or decrease the amount of growth, or to change diets for young fish; 3) assisting in spawning operations; 4) performing activities for the care, feeding and incubation of fertilized eggs; and 5) keeping accurate records such as quantity and formula of feed, mortalities, etc.
 C. I am able to follow routine schedules and work methods to provide for the normal needs of hatchery fish, such as 1) catching and handling fish with nets or hands; 2) providing regular/scheduled feed; 3) recognizing obvious changes to fish appearance and activity such as pregnancy, fighting, or refusal to feed; and 4) keeping records such as fish size, weight, sex, and age
 D. I have the ability to perform dependable work caring for fish under close supervision and would require more than normal supervision to perform them.
