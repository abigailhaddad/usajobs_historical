Thank you for your interest in the WG-5703-06 Motor Vehicle Operator position. We will evaluate your resume and your responses to this Assessment Questionnaire to determine if you are among the best qualified for this position. 

Please read and choose the best one answer that best describes how you meet the qualification requirements as described in the Qualifications Section of the Vacancy Announcement. 

Your responses are subject to verification, so please review your responses for accuracy before you submit this questionnaire.
Select “Yes” or “No” to the following question(s).
1. Ability To Do the Work Without More Than Normal Supervision - Must be skilled in the operation of a multi-passenger, wheelchair equipped vans, and have a current valid Oklahoma Department of Transportation Commercial Driver's License (CDL); must be familiar with National Department of Transportation and Oklahoma and Texas guidelines/policies governing motor vehicle operators and well versed in every aspect of patient safety during transport; required to know office automation duties; work is routine and straightforward, requiring the execution of simple and/or well-defined functions; required to attend job-related skills training as coordinated by the Veterans Transportation Service program; required to know and be certified annually in Basic First Aid, Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR), and protective precautions with communicably transmitted diseases; familiar with the medical equipment in the vehicle and can quickly locate such equipment for accompanying medical technician; skilled in transferring empty oxygen tanks for full tanks, reading gauges, and regulating oxygen flow for patients transport requiring this service during non-accompanied medical technician trips; skilled in driving the vehicle in various roads conditions to safely transport patients to their destination and know how to properly load and secure wheelchair patients in the vehicle; knowledge of vehicles equipped with special-purpose accessory equipment; ability to use the power devices that operate van lifts and skilled in manipulating a variety of controls and understanding equipment gauges, engine fluid level instruments and warning signals; must be familiar with tire chain installation when hazardous, snow packed, and icy road conditions occur; must possess mechanical abilities in determining nature of breakdowns, tire change or repair, etc., enabling the vehicle to be driven to the nearest repair shop or summoning help via available communication systems; provides patient care/support, which is appropriate to the cognitive, emotional and chronological maturation needs of the young adult (18-44 years old), middle adult (45-64 years old), and older adult (65-older patients); and must demonstrate the knowledge and skills necessary to provide care/support appropriate to the age of the patients in the assigned service areas.

Driver is to maintain a current CPR and First Aid Certification and demonstrates the knowledge and skills necessary to provide care appropriate to the age of patients being served and will be required to attend job-related skills and training as coordinated through Veteran Transportation Service program.

Do you possess the ability to do the work as described above without more than normal supervision?
